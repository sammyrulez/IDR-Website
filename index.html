Identify key metrics

  1. Number of Players: Keeping track of the total number of players over time will give you a good indication of how successful your game is and how well it is being received by the public.
  2. Retention Rate: Retention rate is the percentage of players that come back to your game after their initial play session. It is an important metric to track as it indicates the level of engagement with your game and the likelihood of long-term success.
  3. Average Playtime: Average playtime is the average amount of time each player spends playing your game. It is important to track this metric as it shows how long players are staying interested in your game and whether or not they are coming back for more.
  4. Revenue: Revenue is the total amount of money earned from the game. This is one of the most important metrics to track in order to gauge the success of your game and its profitability.
  5. Player Acquisition: Player acquisition is the number of new players that are signing up for your game each month. Tracking this metric gives you an idea of how well your marketing and advertising efforts are performing and whether or not your game is resonating with people.

Engage your users more

  1. Make sure the game is easy to learn and has intuitive controls.
  2. Design data backed new content to keep users engaged.
  3. Offer discounts and special offers to loyal players.
  4. Use analytics to understand what players are playing and what they are not, and adjust accordingly.
